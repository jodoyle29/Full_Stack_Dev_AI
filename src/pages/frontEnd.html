Help for Drawing Pictures

Currently the only form elements that can be recognized are textbox's and their corresponding labels. We hope to introduce new features in the future and give constant updates to make the experience more user friendly. However, this website is experimental and for demo purposes only. With that being said, here are some tips to get the best results with the current model.

-Make sure that the photo is clear and doesnt have any shadows or any other abnomalities that could change results.

-It is best to use blank white paper(avoid lined paper) or a virtual drawing software works as well.

-Pen is prefered over pencil.

Labels are recognized as lines of text. If you want a label to have multiple words keep them on the same line and not to far apart from each other.

Example of a good label, notice how words are on the same line and not spaced too far apart. This will be recongonized as a single label "Last name".

Example of a bad label, notice how words are not on the same line. This will be recongonized as multiple labels "Last" and "name".

Textboxes are recognized as boxes/rectangles. Try to make them as clean as possible with 4 edges, however there is a bit of room for error.

Example of a good textbox, notice how the box is roughly rectangular and has 4 edges. Even though it is not perfect it will still be detectable as a textbox.

Example of bad textboxes, notice how neither of them have 4 edges. These textboxes may or may not be detected so it is best practice to avoid drawing like this.

Labels should be in line and to the left of textboxes you wish to match it with. Only one label can be matched to a certain textbox.

Example of well drawn label-textbox pair. Notice how they are in line with each other and the label is to the left of the textbox.

Example of bad label-textbox pairs, notice how the first pair is not lined up properly. The second pair is lined up, but the label is to the right not left, this will result in undefined behaviour.

When drawing multiple label-textbox pairs it is important to keep the same scale for different pairs and leave some space between the different pairs.

Example of good consistent sizing. Both pairs of label-textbox are roughly to the same scale.

Example of bad and inconsistent sizing. Each label-textbox pair is drawn at a differnt scale and this may lead to undefined behaviour.
